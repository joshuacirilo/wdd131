Antigua Guatemala

History

Antigua was the capital of Guatemala from 1543 to 1776 when a devastating earthquake led to the capital being moved to Guatemala City. The city is a prominent example of Spanish colonial architecture and has been designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Nature

Antigua is situated in a valley surrounded by volcanoes, providing an impressive natural setting. The city features gardens and parks that offer panoramic views of the surrounding volcanoes.

culture

Culture in Antigua is manifested through its architecture, festivals, and cuisine. Holy Week is particularly famous, with processions that attract visitors from around the world. Additionally, the city hosts numerous museums and cultural centers that celebrate Guatemalan history and art.

Tikal

History

Tikal was one of the most powerful cities of the Maya civilization, flourishing between the 6th and 9th centuries AD. During its peak, Tikal served as a political, economic, and military center. The city was abandoned in the 10th century, and its network of temples and palaces was covered by jungle for centuries..

Maya culture is reflected in Tikal's monumental architecture, which includes temples, plazas, and stelae. Religious ceremonies and rituals were integral to life in Tikal, and many of these cultural aspects can be explored through the ruins.
